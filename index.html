Mechanism of Action

How Revora Works

Hydrophilic Matrix

Hyaluronic acid is extremely hydrophilic — it absorbs up to 1,000× its weight in water. When it contacts fluids such as blood or saliva, it increases in viscosity, forming a stable, protective gel matrix over the treatment site.

On a microscopic level, HA binds to specific cell surface receptors, enhancing extracellular stability and creating optimal conditions for regeneration.

Multi-Path Recovery

Revora facilitates periodontal tissue recovery through multiple simultaneous mechanisms:

  • 01 Supports normal cellular infiltration for ordered tissue rebuilding
  • 02 Stabilizes blood clotting to protect the healing environment
  • 03 Establishes a microbial barrier to prevent infection during recovery
  • 04 Promotes fibroblast migration and collagen formation for lasting repair
