The Science Behind Revora

Hyaluronic acid is a naturally occurring biomaterial with decades of clinical validation — now formulated specifically for veterinary periodontal regeneration.

The Active

Hyaluronic Acid in Dentistry

Hyaluronic acid (HA) is a naturally occurring glycosaminoglycan present in connective, epithelial, and neural tissues. In the oral cavity, it contributes to extracellular matrix integrity, tissue hydration, and cellular signaling processes critical for wound healing.

HA's role in periodontal regeneration has been validated across more than 100 human clinical studies and 5+ dedicated canine studies, consistently demonstrating statistically significant efficacy.

Key Properties of HA

  • ◆ Absorbs up to 1,000× its weight in water — extremely hydrophilic
  • ◆ Increases viscosity on contact with blood or saliva
  • ◆ Binds to cell surface receptors, enhancing extracellular stability
  • ◆ Biocompatible, non-cytotoxic, and non-sensitizing
  • ◆ Proven safe for mucosal application in companion animals
Mechanism of Action

How HA Drives Periodontal Recovery

Revora facilitates tissue recovery through multiple simultaneous biological pathways.

Cellular Infiltration

Supports ordered migration of fibroblasts and osteoblasts into the wound site, enabling organized — not chaotic — tissue rebuilding from the cellular level up.

Clot Stabilization

Stabilizes blood clotting at the treatment site, protecting the healing environment and providing a scaffold for new tissue to form upon.

Microbial Barrier

Establishes a physical and biochemical barrier against bacterial infiltration, reducing infection risk during the critical early healing window.

True Regeneration

Beyond Healing — Complete Tissue Restoration

Revora doesn't just facilitate wound closure. It drives organized periodontal regeneration, rebuilding the structures that matter.

Bone Regeneration

Visible bone regrowth in damaged or infected areas, with development of stronger mineral-content bone tissue. Studies demonstrate measurable increases in bone density and volume at periodontal defect sites.

Soft Tissue Regeneration

Accelerated regeneration of periodontal ligaments and cementum. Enhanced attachment strength between teeth and bone. Organized regeneration restores normal anatomy, preventing disordered hyperplastic healing.

Collagen Formation

Promotes fibroblast migration and collagen formation, the structural protein essential for connective tissue integrity. This ensures lasting, durable tissue repair rather than temporary wound closure.

Tissue Hydration

Maintains a moist wound environment optimal for healing through HA's extraordinary hydrophilic properties. Proper tissue hydration supports angiogenesis and accelerates overall recovery timelines.
